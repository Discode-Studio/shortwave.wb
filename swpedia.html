Russia

  • General Information: HF radio networks follow common sets of rules.
  • Message Formats: Morse Code, Phonetic Alphabet.

    • HF Presence of Russian Ground Forces

    • Callsigns: Combinations of letters and digits.
    • Networks:
      • Buzzer (4625 kHz)
      • D Marker (5292 kHz)
      • T Marker (4325 kHz)
      • Air Horn (4930 kHz)
      • Goose (4310/3243 kHz)
      • Alarm (4770 kHz)
      • Katok65 (4224/3218.5 kHz)
      • Zaliv52 (4110 kHz)
    • Southern Military District:
      • The Pip (5448/3756 kHz)
      • The Squeaky Wheel (5367/3363.5 kHz)
      • Vega (5372 kHz)
      • Baron78 (4940/3850 kHz)

    Monolith Morse Networks

    • Description: Hard to research, limited information available.
    • Networks:
      • XHOP (Russian Air Force, Military Transport Aviation, Long-Range Aviation)
      • REA4 (Russian Navy)

    Modes of Communication

    • Two-way communication
    • Weather information and radiogram traffic
    • Digital Modes: T-600, AT-3104
